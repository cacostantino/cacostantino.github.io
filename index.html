About Me

Christine A. Costantino

I’ve been involved in higher education for 25 years as a student, technologist, and project manager. After earning a B.A in psychology from the University of Connecticut, I left higher education to pursue a career in client services, until I discovered the World Wide Web and what its technologies can achieve.

I returned to school to study web design and development at Bryant  College, and thus began my new career as a Web developer. After getting a few years of experience and expanding my technology prowess, I found my way back into higher education as a technologist for the Center for Clinical Trials at Brown University.

I am currently an Assistant Director of Educational Technology in the Center for Teaching and Learning at Yale, where I support faculty with Canvas, and manage pilots and web projects.

I am applying to doctoral programs in educational technology and plan to embark on research and teaching in higher education.
